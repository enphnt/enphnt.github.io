Stereo Imaging

This playlist is all about highlighting songs with great use of stereo image. Big sounds and interesting recording and production techniques are abundant.

Stereo imaging is the process of creating width, depth, and the perception of locality in a music mix. It is used to create a sense of space for the listener. When audio engineers mix a music track in stereo; instrumentals and vocals are layered at different output levels to the left and right channels to create a sense of width, depth and even height.

Songs w/ 🔥 Stereo Image


How Stereo Image can enhance a song:

Let's look into how some of the tracks are using stereo imaging as an effect to enhance and compliment the song.

The Flaming Lips - Race For The Prize

  • Song Name: Race for the Prize
  • Artist: The Flaming Lips
  • Year: 1999
  • Album: The Soft Bulletin

The video is classic but it's really spectacular to hear the song in this live performance with The Colorado Symphony & André de Ridder:

Race for the Prize tells the story of two scientists who are competing to find a cure for a disease, but are willing to sacrifice their lives and families for the greater good. The song is within the neo-psychedelic genre and features lush orchestration and distorted drums.

The song uses stereo imaging to create a wide and dynamic soundstage, with different instruments and vocals panned across the left and right channels. The song also uses reverb and delay to add depth and realism to the mix. The recording complements the theme of scientific exploration and human compassion, as well as using dynamic contrast to accentuate chaotic and serene moments.

The song is praised by critics and fans alike, and was ranked as one of the best songs of the 90s by Pitchfork Media. The song is also a staple of the band's live performances, often featuring giant balloons and confetti.

Wichita Lineman (Remastered 2001)

  • Song Name: Wichita Lineman

  • Year: 1968
  • Album: Wichita Lineman

Wichita Lineman is a country-pop song that tells the story of a lonely telephone lineman who works on the open plains of Kansas. The song was written by Jimmy Webb and produced by Al De Lory. The song features a rich orchestral arrangement and a distinctive guitar solo by Campbell.

The song also uses stereo imaging to create a contrast between the sparse verses and the lush choruses, as well as to emphasize the isolation and longing of the narrator.

It was a huge hit for Campbell and is widely regarded as one of the best songs of all time, and has been covered by many artists such as Johnny Cash, R.E.M., and James Taylor.

David Bowie - Lazarus

  • Song Name: Lazarus
  • Artist: David Bowie
  • Year: 2015
  • Album: Blackstar

Lazarus is a jazz-rock song that was released as the second single from David Bowie's final studio album, Blackstar. The song was written by Bowie and produced by Bowie and Tony Visconti. The song features a haunting saxophone melody and a distorted guitar riff.

This is a pretty amazing breakdown of this song. Leah Kardos goes into painstaking detail analysing the song, the chords, and it's inspirations:

The song also uses stereo imaging to create a dark and atmospheric sound, with Bowie's vocals moving from one channel to another. The dark atmosphere absolutely enhances the lyrical content and overall grim subject matter.

The song was inspired by Bowie's own impending death from cancer, and the lyrics and video of the song are seen as a self-epitaph and a farewell message to his fans.

Carpenters - Superstar

  • Song Name: Superstar
  • Artist: Carpenters
  • Year: 1971
  • Album: Carpenters

Obligatory Tommy Boy reference:

Superstar tells the story of a groupie who is in love with a rock star who has left her behind. The song is a soft rock song that was originally written by Bonnie Bramlett and Leon Russell, and popularized by The Carpenters. The song features Karen Carpenter's smooth and expressive vocals and Richard Carpenter's lush orchestral arrangement.

The song uses stereo imaging to create a warm and intimate sound, with Karen's voice panned slightly to the right and Richard's piano panned slightly to the left. The mix complements the theme of unrequited love and loneliness, as well as the contrast between the glamorous and the mundane aspects of life.

The song was a huge hit for The Carpenters and is considered one of their signature songs. It has been covered by many artists such as Luther Vandross, Sonic Youth, and Usher.

Marvin Gaye - What's Going On

  • Song Name: What's Going On
  • Artist: Marvin Gaye
  • Year: 1971
  • Album: What's Going On

What's Going On is a soul song that was released as the lead single from Marvin Gaye's eleventh studio album of the same name. The song was inspired by Marvin Gaye's brother's experience in the Vietnam War, and the lyrics address social issues such as war, poverty, police brutality, and environmentalism. It was written by Marvin Gaye, Renaldo Benson, and Al Cleveland, and produced by Gaye. Notably, this song features a smooth groove and a saxophone solo by Eli Fontaine.

Here's Marvin Gaye discussing the album and the time he recorded these tracks:

The song uses stereo imaging to create a realistic and immersive sound, with sounds of people talking, laughing, and chanting in the background. The immersive effect complements the theme of social awareness and activism, as well as the dynamic contrast between the peaceful and the violent aspects of society.

The song was a critical and commercial success, reaching number two on the Billboard Hot 100 and number one on the R&B chart. The song is considered one of Gaye's masterpieces and one of the most influential songs of all time, and has been covered by many artists such as Cyndi Lauper, Donny Hathaway, and A Perfect Circle

Boston - Foreplay / Long Time

  • Song Name: Foreplay / Long Time
  • Artist: Boston
  • Year: 1976
  • Album: Boston

It's a song that deserves headbanging in the car with the whole family. The song was actually covered by a rock and metal super group recently which garnered a fair bit of attention:

Foreplay / Long Time is a progressive rock song that consists of two parts: Foreplay, an instrumental introduction, and Long Time, the main song. The song was released as the second single from Boston's debut album, which became one of the best-selling albums of all time. It was written by Tom Scholz, the founder and leader of Boston, and produced by Scholz and John Boylan.

The song features Scholz's signature guitar sound and organ playing, as well as Brad Delp's powerful vocals. The song uses stereo imaging to create a dynamic and spacious sound, with different instruments and effects panned across the left and right channels. The effect complements the theme of taking one's time and moving along in life, as well as the contrast between the calm and the energetic moments.

The song was a hit for Boston and is considered one of their classics as well as one of the greatest rock songs of all time. It has been featured in many video games, movies, and TV shows.

  • Year: 2014
  • Album: Syro

Aphex Twin is one of those rare artists who makes music that can actually scare you. The videos don't usually calm you nerves either:

The song was praised by critics and fans for its intricate and challenging sound. The song is considered one of Aphex Twin's most adventurous and creative songs, and similarly adventurous tracks from this album have been featured in some video games and TV shows.

Lewis - I Thought The World of You

  • Song Name: I Thought the World of You
  • Artist: Lewis
  • Year: 1983
  • Album: L'Amour

I Thought the World of You is a lo-fi pop song that features a soft synth melody and a whispered vocal sample. The song was written and produced by Lewis, a mysterious musician whose real name is Randall Wulff.

The song is considered one of Lewis' most captivating and enigmatic songs. A documentary, "I Thought the World of You" was released in 2022 about Lewis:

The song uses stereo imaging to create a dreamy and nostalgic sound, with the synth and the voice panned slightly to opposite channels. The mix complements the theme of lost love and regret, as well as the contrast between the past and the present.

The song was released as the opening track from L'Amour, a private press album that was discovered in an Edmonton flea market by collector Jon Murphy. The song was praised by critics and fans for its haunting and minimalist beauty.

Toots and the Maytals - Pressure Drop

Pressure Drop is an essential reggae song that issues a warning to anyone who does bad things to innocent people. The title is a phrase that singer Frederick “Toots” Hibbert used to say, meaning that if someone wronged him, he would not fight them but let karma take care of them. The song was inspired by Toots' own experience of being arrested and thrown in jail for a year for something he did not do. It was recorded by Toots and the Maytals for record producer Leslie Kong.

The song uses stereo imaging to create a lively and energetic sound, with different instruments and vocals panned across the left and right channels. In this song, the technique enhances the catchy groove and the powerful vocal performance, as well as the contrast between the peaceful and the violent aspects of karmic justice discussed in the lyrics.

The song helped launch the band's career outside Jamaica when it was featured on the soundtrack to the 1972 film The Harder They Come, which introduced reggae to much of the world. The song has been covered by many artists such as Robert Palmer, The Specials, Keith Richards, and The Clash.

Nick Cave & The Bad Seeds - Red Right Hand

  • Song: Red Right Hand
  • Artist: Nick Cave & The Bad Seeds
  • Year: 1994
  • Album: Let Love In

Obligatory Dumb and Dumber reference:

The song is a dark and ominous country ballad that depicts a mysterious and powerful figure who stalks the land and offers dubious deals to people. Nick Cave recorded the song with his band The Bad Seeds at Wessex Sound Studios in London, England, in 1993.

The song has a rich and immersive sonic landscape that draws the listener into its dark and mysterious world. There is a sense of space and contrast between different elements of the music, such as the low drone, the synth organ, the vocals, the drums, the guitar, the bass, and the prominent sound effects. The unique panning of these elements creates movement, contrast, depth, width, balance, and variety in the sound. The stereo imaging of the song complements the song by creating a feeling of unease, anticipation, dissonance, menace, and unpredictability that matches the mood and themes of the lyrics.

The song has been covered by various artists, such as:

  • Snoop Dogg, who adopted the persona of "Snoop Shelby" and made a video inspired by the TV show Peaky Blinders, which uses the song as its theme.
  • Arctic Monkeys, who performed the song live several times and recorded a studio version for their 2009 EP Crying Lightning.
  • PJ Harvey, who collaborated with Nick Cave on several songs and albums and recorded the song for the fifth season of Peaky Blinders.

XTC - Life Begins At The Hop

  • Song: Life Begins At The Hop
  • Artist: XTC
  • Year: 1979
  • Album: Drums and Wires

Life Begins At The Hop is a pop rock and new wave song that celebrates dancing and having fun. The song title references the 1950s' rock 'n' roll classic “At the Hop” by Danny and the Juniors. It was the first single by XTC to feature Dave Gregory on guitar.

Here they are performing the song at the Top of the Pops in 1979:

The song uses stereo imaging to create a sense of energy and excitement. The synth riff, guitar, vocals, drums, bass, and sound effects are panned in different ways to create movement, contrast, depth, width, balance, and variety. The energy and dynamics in the stereo image of the song aids the narrative and lyrical theme on dancing and having fun.

The song was used in the soundtrack of the 1998 film The Wedding Singer, starring Adam Sandler and Drew Barrymore. It was covered by The Dukes of Stratosphear, a psychedelic rock side project of XTC, on their 1987 album Psonic Psunspot.

The Beatles - The End (Remastered 2009)

  • Song: The End (Remastered 2009)
  • Artist: The Beatles
  • Year: 1969
  • Album: Abbey Road

The song is the final track of Abbey Road and features a medley of solos from John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr.

Jack Black and KG gave this track a whirl not too long ago. It's a pretty fun reimagining:

There are some other notable covers of The Beatles - The End, just like alot of their tracks, some of them are from: Phil Collins and Neil Diamond.

The song uses balanced panning to create a sense of space and clarity between different elements of the music, such as the piano, guitar, vocals, drums, bass, and orchestral instruments. The stereo imaging of the song showcases the Beatles' recording techniques in this recording, such as using:

  • automatic double tracking (ADT) to create a fuller and richer vocal sound.
  • direct injection (DI) to record the bass guitar directly into the mixing console, bypassing the amplifier and microphone.
  • tape loops and vari-speed to create the orchestral effects and timpani sounds.
  • close miking and compression to capture the drum sound.
  • overdubbing and editing to create the seamless transition between the solos.
